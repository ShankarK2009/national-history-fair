# APUSH History Fair Project Guide

## Project Overview

The History Fair offers a creative, hands-on approach to developing reading, writing, and reasoning skills. You will choose a topic related to the annual theme, conduct primary and secondary research, and present your work in the form of an **exhibit, website, or documentary.**

**Theme:** Revolution, Reaction, & Reform in History

---

## Project Requirements

### 1. Written & Visual Content

- **Word Count:** Minimum of **1,000 words** of text.
- **Visuals:** Minimum of **12–15 images** (primary and secondary source photographs, maps, charts, political cartoons, timelines, etc.).
- **Tone & Quality:** Text must be clear, concise, and free of spelling, or grammatical errors. Design and color choices should enhance readability and be appropriate to the topic.

### 2. Essential Elements

Your project must include the following sections under specific subheadings:

- **Catchy/Creative Title:** A unique title for your project.
- **Complex Thesis:** A clear, historically defensible argument responding to your research question. It must include keywords related to the theme (**Revolution, Reaction, & Reform**) and may be more than one sentence.
- **Historical Context/Background:** The time and place of your topic.
- **Detailed Description of Topic:** Immediate causes, details of the event, and immediate effects/impact. Include multiple perspectives/viewpoints where applicable.
- **Legacy:** The long-term significance of the topic in regards to the theme of revolution, reaction, and reform.

### 3. Bibliography

- **Format:** MLA Formatting.
- **Source Count:**
  - **Individual Projects:** Minimum of 7 primary and secondary sources.
  - **Group Projects:** Minimum of 10 primary and secondary sources.
- **Inclusions:** Must include all primary sources and additional sources for visuals.
- **Submission:** A printed copy (or embedded version for websites) must accompany the presentation.

---

## Rubric & Grading Criteria

| Category                | Expectations                                                                                                                                                      |
| :---------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Thesis**              | Makes a clear, analytical argument with specific, relevant subclaims. Directly connects to the "Revolution, Reaction, Reform" theme.                              |
| **Historical Evidence** | Information is contextualized, accurate, and credible. No critical omissions. Argument is consistently supported by evidence and analysis (explaining "how/why"). |
| **Written & Visuals**   | 1000+ words. 12-15 high-quality visuals. Professional organization, clear structure, and appropriate design choices.                                              |
| **Bibliography**        | Meets source minimums (7 individual / 10 group). Accurate MLA formatting.                                                                                         |
