Research Question

How did violent backlash shape Reconstruction's collapse?

How did the Ku Klux Klan's violent backlash against Radical Reconstruction's legal reforms, such as the 14th and 15th Amendments and federal enforcement laws, attempt to reverse post Civil War gains for Black Americans, and how did this resistance contribute both to the collapse of Reconstruction's civil rights protections and to the long-term direction of future civil rights policy in the United States?

Thesis

Federal reform met organized intimidation.

Although Radical Reconstruction attempted a constitutional revolution through the 14th and 15th Amendments, the Ku Klux Klan's violent reaction overwhelmed federal enforcement efforts and successfully intimidated Black citizens into political submission. This systemic terror not only collapsed post-war civil rights protections but established a legacy of disenfranchisement that deferred true democratic reform until the Civil Rights Movement of the 1960s.
